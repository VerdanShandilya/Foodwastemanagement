Food Management Tips

Meal Planning, Storage, and Preparation

Here are some tips to help you plan, store, and prepare your meals:

  • Plan your meals for the week ahead of time to avoid last-minute stress and unhealthy choices.
  • Buy fresh fruits and vegetables in season and in bulk to save money and reduce waste.
  • Store fruits and vegetables in the refrigerator in separate bins to prevent spoilage.
  • Use resealable containers to store leftovers and label them with the date so you know when to eat them.
  • Cook large batches of food and freeze them in individual portions for easy meals later on.
  • Prep your ingredients in advance, such as chopping vegetables, to save time during meal prep.

Composting

Composting is a great way to reduce food waste and create nutrient-rich soil for your garden. Here are some tips to get started:

  • Choose a composting bin or container that fits your space and needs.
  • Add a mixture of brown and green materials, such as leaves and vegetable scraps, to your compost pile.
  • Turn your compost pile regularly to aerate it and speed up the decomposition process.
  • Avoid adding meat, dairy, and oils to your compost pile, as they can attract pests and slow down decomposition.

Animal Feeding

Some food waste can be used to feed animals, either through backyard composting or through commercial operations that collect food scraps for animal feed.

  • Food waste that is not suitable for human consumption can still be used as animal feed.
  • This can include vegetable and fruit trimmings, expired food products, and bakery waste.
  • Food waste can be fed to a variety of animals, including pigs, chickens, and cows.
  • Before feeding food waste to animals, it should be sorted to remove any non-food items and ensure that it is safe and healthy for the animals to consume.

Food Recovery Programs

Food recovery programs help to reduce food waste and provide nutritious meals to those in need. Here are some ways you can get involved:

  • Donate excess food from your pantry or fridge to a local food bank or food recovery program.
  • Volunteer at a local food bank or soup kitchen to help prepare and serve meals.
  • Support businesses that donate excess food to food recovery programs.
  • Spread awareness about food waste and the importance of food recovery programs in your community.
